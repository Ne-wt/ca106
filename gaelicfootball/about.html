About Gaelic football

Gaelic football is played among two 15-aside teams. Each team consists of a goalkeeper, six defenders, six attackers, and two midfielders. The teams are lined out in a grid corresponding with the numbers on the back of their shirt


The goalposts in Gaelic football are similar to those in rugby, only that the crossbar is lower and the posts are slightly wider. A shot that goes over the bar is worth one point and a shot that goes under the bar is worth three, this is called a goal. The minimum size for a Gaelic pitch is 30m longer than a rugby pitch and 10m wider.


County teams across the country compete for various titles. At the start of the year, teams compete in their divisions. There are four divisions in Gaelic football, each with eight teams. 31 counties are represented along with London, the only English team in the competition. Kilkenny is unique as they don’t compete in the All-Ireland series.


Teams then compete for the provincial championships with London and New York playing in Connaught. The winner of these 4 championships then progresses to the all-Ireland round robbin. This round consists of eight teams split into two groups where the top two teams in each group go through. The All-Ireland final is down to be played in the second last week in July and is played in Croke Park, the home of the GAA.


Each county also has its own championship played amongst its own club teams. The winner of these club championships advances to their provincial championship. The All-Ireland club series is a straight knockout. The All-Ireland final is played on st Patrick’s day in Croke Park.
