What is Soccer?

Rules and Understanding.

Brief explanation

Soccer, also called football, is a sport in which two teams of 11 players, using any part of their bodies except their hands and arms, try to put the ball into the opposing teamâ€™s goal. Only the goalkeeper is allowed to handle the ball and may only do so within the penalty area surrounding the goal. The team that scores the most goals wins.

Fouls and Punishments

If a player goes to tackle an opposing team's player and misses the ball, the opposing team will get a freekick. If the tackle is deemed reckless and puts the other player in danger, the tackler will either be given a yellow card or a red card. If two yellow cards are accumulated in a match, this means that the player is shown a red card. When the referee shows a player a red card, the player is banned from the match and must leave the pitch immediately. This means the player's team has only 10 players. The player also misses the next 3 matches if shown a red card.


Photo by Omar Ram

If a foul happens to the attacking team in the opposition's box, a penalty kick is given. The foul could be anything from a reckless tackle to a handball. A penalty kick is when the ball is put down on the penalty spot and a player is able to take a shot on goal. The goalkeeper can save it, but since the penalty spot is so close to the goal it is quite difficult.


Photo by Jeffrey F Lin

If one of the opposing team's players kicks the ball out of play, a throw-in is given to the other team. This means that a player gets to throw the ball from the position where the ball went out of play.
