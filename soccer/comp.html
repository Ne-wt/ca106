Famous Competitions

World Cup, Euros and More

World Cup

The FIFA World Cup is hands down the most famous soccer competition ever. According to sportingintelligence.com, the World Cup has attracted an average of 3.2 billion viewers in 2010 and 2014, making it the most-watched overall live event by average on television worldwide in the 21st century. The competition consists of teams from around the world who play each other in different groups. Each group winner reaches the knockout rounds. Teams play eachother until there are only two left. The winner of the final is the winner of the cup. The last World Cup was in 2018 and France won it against Croatia. However, there was some controversy in the semi-final when France played Belgium because the referee was meant to give more added time but he did not so Belgium lost when they were on the front foot for that part of the game. Euros

The European Championship is similar to the World Cup in the sense that it starts off with a group stage and advances to knockouts. It is arguably harder to win the Euros than the World Cup because Europe has so many high quality teams. Although the World Cup is the more prestigious competion, personally I believe that the Euros is actually more difficult to win. Copa America

The Copa America is basically the South American version of the Euros. The teams from South America play against eachother in the same fashion as the Euros. It does not have as high quality soccer as the World Cup or Euros because there are only a few good teams in South America such as Brazil and Argentina. Teams such as Uruguay and Chile have been good in the past but are declining now. Argentina won the last Copa America, handing Lionel Messi his first ever competition win with his country.
